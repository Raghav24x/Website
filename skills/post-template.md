# post-template

Generate all article-specific copy for the Cash & Cache post templates in one shot. Paste your article (or a summary) after the command and receive three ready-to-drop blocks.

---

## Activation

`/post-template`

Paste the full article text, a working draft, or a bullet-point summary after the command. The more you give, the more specific the output.

---

## Output format

Produce three labelled blocks, in order, separated by a horizontal rule. No preamble. No explanation. Just the copy.

---

### BLOCK 1 — Article Hook (Template 1)

**Purpose:** The variable 1–2 sentences that sit below the separator in the identity block. Opens the reader's mind before the article begins. Not a summary — a crack in the door.

**Rules:**
- Capture the tension, the stakes, or the question the article resolves
- Do not describe what the article covers — make the reader feel something first
- Practitioner-to-practitioner voice: direct, no hype, no "in today's fast-paced AI landscape"
- 1–2 sentences maximum. Shorter is better.
- Past or present tense. Never future-speculative ("soon", "will change", "is coming")

**Examples of the right register:**
- "The workflow everyone talks about is the one nobody has running yet."
- "There's a version of this that takes 40 minutes. Most people are doing the 4-hour version without knowing it."
- "The gap isn't capability. It's the 20 minutes between reading about it and actually running it."

---

### BLOCK 2 — Mid-Article Premium Line (Template 2 Short)

**Purpose:** One sentence that connects this specific article's topic to what lives in the Library. Leads into the fixed CTA "Get full access →".

**Rules:**
- Name the specific thing from the Library that's relevant (template, skill file, framework, guide)
- Do not use generic phrases like "all our resources" or "everything we've built"
- Should feel like a natural extension of the article, not an interruption
- One sentence. No more.

**Format to output:**
> *[One sentence]* **Get full access →**

---

### BLOCK 3 — Contextual Resource Spotlight (Template 4)

**Purpose:** Point the reader to the single most relevant Cash & Cache Library resource based on what the article covers. Specific beats generic every time.

**Rules:**
- Pick exactly one resource from the library below
- Write one sentence explaining why it's relevant to *this specific article* — not what the resource is in general
- If two resources tie, pick the free one (lower barrier for new readers)
- Format exactly as shown below

**Format to output:**
> **From the Library:** [Resource title] — *[One sentence on why it's relevant to what you just read.]* →

## Voice guidelines (apply to all blocks)

- **Practitioner to practitioner** — not teacher to student, not marketer to consumer
- **Specific over generic** — name the thing, don't describe the category
- **Implementation-first** — what this helps you DO, not what it makes you THINK
- **No hype phrases** — ban: "game-changing", "revolutionize", "supercharge", "unlock your potential", "in today's world"
- **Tense** — present or past. Never speculative future.
- **Length** — each block should feel tight. If you can cut a word, cut it.
